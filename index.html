FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

What is the best season to visit Kerala backwaters?

October to March is the peak season with pleasant weather (20-32°C), clear skies, and calm waters - ideal for photography and outdoor activities. April to May is hot but less crowded with good discounts. June to September (monsoon) offers lush green landscapes, cooler temperatures, and a peaceful atmosphere with fewer tourists. Each season has its unique charm, so choose based on your preference for weather versus tranquility.

How is the pricing determined and what does it include?

Pricing varies based on: number of guests (2-8 people), cruise type (day cruise 6 hours or overnight 21 hours), food preferences (vegetarian/non-vegetarian/seafood specialties), season (peak/off-peak rates), and houseboat category (standard/deluxe/premium). All packages include: meals as per duration, welcome drinks, tea and snacks, experienced multilingual crew, safety equipment, life jackets, and fuel. AC charges are additional for overnight stays. Contact us via WhatsApp or call for a detailed quote customized to your group size and preferences.

Can you accommodate dietary restrictions and food preferences?

Absolutely! We cater to all dietary needs including vegetarian, vegan, Jain food (no onion/garlic), gluten-free, and specific allergies. Our chefs prepare authentic Kerala cuisine with fresh local ingredients - fish curry, karimeen pollichathu, appam, Kerala parotta, and traditional meals served on banana leaves. We can also prepare North Indian, Chinese, or continental dishes upon request. Inform us of your preferences when booking, and we'll customize the entire menu to ensure everyone enjoys delicious, safe meals.

How far in advance should I book?

We recommend booking 1-2 weeks in advance during peak season (October to March) and holidays like Christmas, New Year, and Onam festival when demand is highest. For off-peak months (April to September), 3-5 days advance booking is usually sufficient. However, we often accommodate last-minute bookings based on availability. Contact us on WhatsApp for instant availability updates - we respond quickly and can sometimes arrange same-day bookings if houseboats are available.

What languages does your crew speak?

Our experienced crew is multilingual and fluent in Malayalam (local language), English, Hindi, and Tamil. This ensures comfortable communication for guests from all regions of India and international visitors. Our crew can explain local culture, point out interesting sights along the route, share stories about village life, and ensure all your needs are understood and met throughout your journey.

What should I bring for the houseboat cruise?

Essentials to pack: comfortable cotton clothing, sunscreen (SPF 30+), sunglasses, hat or cap, camera/phone for photos, personal medications, and mosquito repellent. For overnight stays: light sleepwear, toiletries, and a light jacket for cooler evenings. We provide: clean bedding, towels, basic toiletries, and life jackets. The houseboats have fans and optional AC. Avoid heavy luggage - keep it light and casual for a relaxed backwater experience.
